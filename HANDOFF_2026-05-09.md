**Kelly sizing (with Kalshi taker fees):**
- Fee per contract = `0.07 × p × (1-p)` on entry; settlement is fee-free; we hold to settle so one-sided fee
- For YES at price p with our_p q: `b = (1-p-fee)/(p+fee)`, `f* = (b·q − (1-q))/b`
- Half-Kelly applied (`KELLY_FRACTION = 0.5`)
- Per-bet cap: 5% of live bankroll
- Total exposure cap: 50% of live bankroll across all open
- Min 5 contracts, max 500 contracts per single position
- Bankroll = $5,000 + sum(realized P&L from closed T6 positions), recomputed every cycle

**KL divergence parallel calc (logged not triggered):** Each signal's metadata records `kl_nats = sharp·ln(sharp/kalshi) + (1-sharp)·ln((1-sharp)/(1-kalshi))` with prob-clip [0.01, 0.99]. ## Critical Operational Lessons

### Net new from 2026-05-08 / 2026-05-09

**13. Kalshi macro daily markets have no flow.** The T7 concept (10Y/WTI/DXY daily close) was killed by direct probing: `volume_24h = 0` across every macro market sampled, even those with deep MM books. The flow on Kalshi clusters around political events, sports, and the same monthly/weekly macro releases T3b/T3c already trade. Probe `/markets/trades` before building any new engine — `volume_24h` lies.

**14. Sports markets ARE the high-cadence path.** KXMLBGAME shows 45 active events/day, real retail flow (12 trades/min on at-the-money), tight spreads (1-7¢), 2 markets per game (clean binary). The 5-book Vegas consensus from The Odds API gives us the leading indicator. T6 is the pivot that works.

**15. Kelly sizing without fee modeling is wrong.** Kalshi's quadratic taker fee `0.07 × p × (1-p)` per contract on entry meaningfully shifts the optimal Kelly fraction. At a 5pp edge on a 50¢ market, full-Kelly drops from 0.10 to 0.067 — a 33% size reduction. Always include fees in the per-trade `b` (net odds) computation.

**16. Bankroll for Kelly must float.** Hardcoded bankroll loses meaning as equity accumulates. T6 recomputes bankroll = INITIAL + sum(realized P&L) every cycle. Caps (per-bet 5%, total exposure 50%) scale dynamically.

**17. WebSocket > REST for active books.** REST polling at 5min cadence misses sub-minute book moves on liquid markets. WS subscribes to `orderbook_delta` + `trade` channels for streaming sub-100ms updates. Required auth via signed RSA-PSS-SHA256 headers. T6 cut over to WS this session — same architecture as `kalshi_shadow_ws.py`. Snapshot writes every 5 seconds keep the on-disk format compatible with paper trader and dashboard.

**18. Entropy collapse detection requires a liquidity gate.** Single retail orders on thin macro books produce false-positive entropy z-scores. Two-condition AND: `|z| > 3` AND `5min_volume > rolling_median × 1.5`. Within-event rolling baselines (not cross-event) handle the 12-events/yr problem on T3b.

**19. Defensive entropy gate before offensive.** Phase 1: refuse fading-collapse entries (don't sell into informed flow). Phase 2: enter WITH collapse direction only after n≥15 calibration alerts confirm hit rate > 65%. The first 15 alerts must be observed, not acted on directionally.

**20. Linear delta is correct for EV; KL is correct for noise normalization.** EV per dollar = `q - p` (linear). KL captures information content of the gap, normalizing for distance from 50/50. Use linear for triggering until empirical KL distribution at n=50 says otherwise. The literature thresholds (KL=0.05/0.10 in some sources) are too restrictive on natural-log scale; calibrate from your own ledger.

**21. macOS Terminal closure can kill nohup'd processes.** When you close a Terminal tab and click "Terminate" on the warning, macOS sends a kill signal stronger than SIGHUP. nohup doesn't help. To preserve daemons: type `exit` first to clean-exit the shell, THEN close the window. If the warning appears, click "Don't Terminate." Or use deploy scripts after every session to verify daemon state.
