## The deeper finding (kills the engine)

The handoff hypothesis was that "ADP is just another noisy nowcast." Stronger result: **ADP has near-zero predictive power for NFP, in any era.**

### Correlation ρ(NFP m/m, ADP m/m)

| Era | n | ρ |
|---|---|---|
| Legacy 2014-2019 | 72 | **0.20** |
| Stanford 2023-now | 40 | **0.34** |

Both pathetic. Even with optimal linear conditioning on ADP:
- Legacy: residual σ = 80K (only 4% variance reduction vs unconditional NFP)
- Stanford: residual σ = 111K (only 6% variance reduction)

### Variance explained by ADP (using crude NFP − ADP residual)

| Era | σ(NFP) unconditional | σ(NFP − ADP) | Variance explained |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legacy 2014-2019 | 82.0K | 92.2K | **−26%** |
| Stanford 2023-now | 118.2K | 135.9K | **−32%** |

**Negative variance explained.** Subtracting ADP from NFP makes the residual *more* volatile than NFP itself. ADP+NFP behave largely as independent random variables; ADP's noise dominates whatever signal it carries.

A regression-conditioned estimator (E[NFP|ADP] = α + β·ADP) is marginally better than the crude residual but still trivially better than the unconditional mean.

## Verdict

**T8 (ADP-based NFP nowcast) — ARCHIVE pre-deployment.**

The architecture is not the problem. The data is. ADP does not produce a leading indicator with structural advantage over the unconditional NFP distribution. Building T8 around ADP residuals would deploy capital on a relationship that empirically does not exist.

Per the handoff's own engine filter ("independent leading indicator outside Kalshi AND costly enough that retail can't easily access it"), ADP fails on both:
- ADP is **publicly free** — no access cost moat
- ADP is **not a meaningful leading indicator** for NFP (ρ < 0.35)

This is the same archetype-failure pattern as T9-with-Deribit-IV (handoff Lesson #27): a free indicator that erodes fast or never had signal.

## Methodology caveats

- **First-print vs revised data.** This study uses FRED's revised PAYEMS values, not BLS first-print. First-print residuals are slightly larger (BLS revisions average 30-50K). Conclusion is unchanged.
- **ADP-Stanford backfill.** Pre-2022 ADPMNUSNERSA values are NOT what ADP printed in real-time; they're back-fits. The legacy clean-era result uses NPPTTL (the actual real-time legacy series). Stanford-era result uses ADPMNUSNERSA as printed (n=40 since 2023-01).
- **Only 40 observations in the Stanford era.** σ confidence interval is wide. Even at the lower 95% bound for σ_stanford, residuals exceed 100K — still 2x bin spacing.
- **No regime stratification.** Did not condition by NFP magnitude or labor-market cycle. Optimistic stratification might extract a bit more edge, but starting from ρ=0.34 leaves little room.
